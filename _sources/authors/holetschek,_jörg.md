## Advancing FAIR Data Management in Livestock: Towards Standardized Metadata

Fischer, Sarah O., Holetschek, Jörg, Melzer, Nina

Published 2024-08-15

Licensed cc-by-4.0



The field of farm animals is embarking on a journey towards FAIR (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable) data management, which is also relevant to the 3R (reduce, replace, refine) principle for animal testing. However, metadata standardisation remains a major challenge, as it is often overlooked due to the absence of a commonly accepted standard in the livestock community. As a starting point and regarding the species-specific projects PIGWEB and IGG, we focus mainly on phenotypic data. The FBN is also part of the NFDI4Biodiversity project. In this context,&nbsp; a comparative analysis of established and ratified standards was conducted. ABCD standard proved a promising candidate for establishing standardised metadata, as it is very flexible and allows the integration of missing terms. Moreover, the ABCD may also serve as a basis for other livestock data. This work highlights the imperative of adopting standardised metadata practices to enhance FAIR data in respect to accessibility, reusability and interoperability.

## NFDI4Biodiversity - Status Report Use Case Projects

Gemeinholzer, Birgit, Frenzel, Mark, Bonn, Aletta, Fischer, Sarah, Holetschek, Jörg, Engel, Thore, Friedrichs-Manthey, Martin, Schomburg, Christoph, Ebert, Barbara

Published 2023-09-07

Licensed cc-by-4.0



Strengthening Germany&#39;s Biodiversity Data Network: User Engagement in NFDI4Biodiversity

The availability of biodiversity data&nbsp;is vital for informed decisions on sustainable use and conservation of biodiversity. NFDI4Biodiversity focuses on enabling biodiversity data exchange among stakeholders.

In collaboration with 50 partner institutions, NFDI4Biodiversity addresses diverse biodiversity facets, driven by partner needs. This report covers a 2.5-year user engagement phase, emphasizing 26 use cases adhering to FAIR principles for data mobilization and integration. The report highlights challenges in networking due to varying stakeholder needs and the need for integration in the biodiversity data landscape.

This report outlines NFDI4Biodiversity&#39;s efforts to strengthen Germany&#39;s biodiversity data network through user engagement, underscoring the importance of collaboration in biodiversity understanding and conservation.

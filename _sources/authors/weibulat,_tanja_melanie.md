## Continuation of partnership Germany-Kenya with two workshops in 2023

Weibulat, Tanja Melanie, Triebel, Dagmar

Published 2024-06-18

Licensed cc-by-4.0



The scientific partnership between the GBIF Participant Nodes of Germany and Kenya started in 2022. It was continued in 2023 with two workshops at the International Training Centre for Environmental Research e.V. (ITCER). One was an hybrid workshop entitled &lsquo;Diversity Workbench (DWB) Training for Scientists: Mobilizing Ethnobiological Data in Africa' and the second was an expert workshop on &lsquo;GBIF Data Publication in Africa&rsquo;. NFDI4Biodiversity was involved. The partners on site include data experts from GBIF Germany and GBIF Kenya as well as members of the International Training Centre for Environmental Research e.V. (ITCER). Additionally, scientists from the environmental department of the County Government of Siaya joined the events and contributed with their regional expertise on environmental change. Some ideas for future activities are discussed.

## Diversity Workbench Training Service in NFDI

Weibulat, Tanja Melanie, Bingert, Sven, Link, Anton, Seifert, Stefan, Triebel, Dagmar

Published 2024-07-23

Licensed cc-by-4.0



The poster was presented at the NFDI4Biodiversity All Hands Conference in Munich 2024 (https://www.nfdi4biodiversity.org/en/news/all-hands-conference-2024/).&nbsp;
It is describing the technical background of the data management system Diversity Workbench (DWB; www.diversityworkbench.net) as well as the key domains and data types which it is focusing on. It is available for free and especially appropriate for project data repositories with a long-term perspective.&nbsp;
The DWB Training service at NFDI is a core service of the GFBio e.V. and part of the NFDI4Biodiversity service catalogue. It is provided in cooperation with the two NFDI partner organisations GWDG and SNSB.&nbsp;&nbsp;
The &nbsp;&bdquo;Software as a Service (SaaS )&ldquo; and &bdquo;Virtual Research Environment (VRE) for Training&ldquo; &nbsp;is accessible via the NFDI4Biodiversity / GFBio Portal Login.

## GBIF Germany Activities 2025

Weibulat, Tanja Melanie, Triebel, Dagmar

Published 2025-07-24

Licensed cc-by-4.0



This lecture was part of the agenda of the 17th meeting of the participant nodes from GBIF Europe and Central Asia (GBIF ECA) and held by German node staff members in Waterford, Ireland (20 May 2025 - 23 May 2025). It describes the situation of the Global Information Facility (GBIF) in Germany in relation to a distributed national network of established data centers at recognized life and nature science organisations. The federated GBIF-D Node system in Germany works technically and organizationally closely together with the German National Research Data Infrastructure (NFDI), GFBio e.V. and NFDI4Biodiversity consortium (supported by the German Research Foundation DFG under the grant agreement number 442032008). This cooperation started in 2013 and facilitates activities in data hosting, data mobilisation and preservation. It is strengthening the efforts of the node to train data management and build informatics capacity, e.g., at the International Training Centre &nbsp;for Environmental Research, ITCER Kenya.

## GFBio Data Centers at NFDI4Biodiversity

Engel, Judith Sophie, Weibulat, Tanja Melanie

Published 2024-06-18

Licensed cc-by-4.0



The poster was presented at the NFDI4Biodiversity All Hands Conference in Munich 2024 (https://www.nfdi4biodiversity.org/en/news/all-hands-conference-2024/). It is explaining&nbsp;the role of the ten GFBio Data Centers in the NFDI4Biodiversity consortium. This is done by&nbsp;visualising first aspects of their profiles and services to make the overview more attractive for&nbsp;a broad user community. All domain-specific data centers have established data repositories in place which are committed on the management and publication of biodiversity and biodiversity-related data. Functions on long-term data preservation are either included or fulfilled by assigned data archiving services. Some of the data centers are more focused on their role as data publishers than others, some have policies in place to eventually accept the long-term storage of physical specimens and material samples as well as multimedia files as special research outputs. Details on the role and services of the single data centers are found under&nbsp;https://www.nfdi4biodiversity.org/en/datenzentren/ and https://kb.gfbio.org/display/KB/Data+Centers.

## Neue Wege zur FAIRen Archivierung von digitalen Forschungsdaten aus Bayern

Weibulat, Tanja Melanie

Published 2024-06-25

Licensed cc-by-4.0



Die &bdquo;NFDI4Biodiversity All Hands Conference&ldquo; ist die j&auml;hrlich stattfindende zentrale Veranstaltung f&uuml;r alle Mitarbeitenden des NFDI4Biodiversity Konsortiums und eingeladene Interessierte. Sie fand 2024 vom 24. bis 26. April in den R&auml;umlichkeiten der Generaldirektion der Staatlichen Archive Bayerns in M&uuml;nchen statt (https://nfdi4biodiversity.org/de/events/all-hands-conference-2024/). Als Konferenzvorprogramm gab es ein Vernetzungstreffen f&uuml;r Angeh&ouml;rige Bayerischer Beh&ouml;rden und amtlicher Stellen im Umfeld der Staatlichen Archive.
Der Vortrag &bdquo;Neue Wege zur FAIRen Archivierung von digitalen Forschungsdaten aus Bayern&ldquo; wurde in diesem Rahmen gehalten. Er beschreibt eine seit 2022 bestehende Kooperation zweier NFDI4Biodiversity-Partner aus Bayern: Die Staatlichen Naturwissenschaftlichen Sammlungen Bayerns (SNSB) und die Generaldirektion der Staatlichen Archive Bayerns (GDA). Zun&auml;chst werden die sich erg&auml;nzenden Aufgaben der beiden Fachorganisationen beschrieben, wobei auf die unterschiedlichen Archivalien und auf die Bedeutung, den Umfang und das Alter der naturhistorischen Forschungssammlungen in Bayern, speziell f&uuml;r den Bereich Biodiversit&auml;tsforschung, hingewiesen wird.&nbsp;
Die laufende Zusammenarbeit besteht zwischen Mitarbeitenden des Data Centers bzw. der Biodiversit&auml;tsinformatik-Abteilung der SNSB und dem digitalen Archiv der GDA. Ziel ist die regelm&auml;&szlig;ige Archivierung der SNSB-Sammlungsdatenbest&auml;nde, wie sie im in-house Datenbanksystem der Diversity Workbench (DWB; www.diversityworkbench.net) gemanagt werden. Dies soll anhand des SIARD-Standards und im SIARD-Datenformat geschehen. Entsprechende Datenfl&uuml;sse, die von der GDA akzeptierte Abgabestruktur f&uuml;r ein SIARD-Datenpaket sowie die Implementierung f&uuml;r einen ausgew&auml;hlten Datenbestand aus dem Pilzherbarium der SNSB werden vorgestellt. 
Die Kooperation soll in der laufenden Phase sowie Phase 2 von NFDI4Biodiversity fortgesetzt werden, um weitere DWB-Datenbest&auml;nde aus verschiedenen Forschungssammlungen (Fossilien, Tiere) zu archivieren und den SIARD-Ansatz auch auf andere relationale Datenbanksysteme auszuweiten. &Uuml;ber die etablierten Netzwerke und Zugriffsportale der staatlichen Archive in Deutschland k&ouml;nnen dann auch ansonsten nicht ver&ouml;ffentliche sensible Bereiche der archivierten Daten (gesch&uuml;tzt durch Personenrecht, Umweltrecht etc.) unter eingeschr&auml;nkten Zugriffsrechten (pseudo-) anonymisiert bereitgestellt werden.

## FROM THE PERSPECTIVE OF THE SAFETY NET. Documentation of the art residency and exhibition 2023 organised by iDiv, UFZ and NFDI4Biodiversity

Köpke, Robert, Wolf, Sophie, Koppernock, Maximilian, Engel,Thore

Published 2023-09-06

Licensed cc-by-4.0



In spring 2023, the German Centre for Integrative Biodiversity Research (iDiv) Halle-Jena-Leipzig and the Helmholtz Center for Environmental Research (UFZ) invited four transdisciplinary conceptual artists &ndash; Snow Paik, Steph Joyce, Nike K&uuml;hn, and Julie Hart &ndash; to delve deeper into their artistic exploration of biodiversity and ecological research during a three-month artist residency. The exhibition &ldquo;From the Perspective of the Safety Net&rdquo; showcased the results of the collaboration between the residency artists and various iDiv scientists from multiple research fields, spanning investigations into physical and mental health benefits of biodiversity on humans to global macroecology of invasive plant species. The project was enabled by NFDI4Biodiversity.

This Zenodo publication contains the exhibition catalogue which documents the residency, the exhibition and the artworks.

## Geovisualization for Biodiversity Data

Köpke, Robert

Published 2022-10-12



EN

As part of the NFDI4Biodiversity All Hands Conference, October 12-14, 2022, Use Case 19 provided an overview of geovisualization approaches in the poster session. In Use Case 19, the goal is to make datasets comprehensible. Spatial visualizations are used to present complex information about biodiversity data in an understandable and transparent way. The work focused on interrogating, analyzing, and linking datasets to reveal new relationships. Special attention is given to addressing different user groups, the quality of the data, and the visualization of uncertainties. The Data Story format offers the possibility to combine different data sets and thus to convey complex information in a clear way.

Licensed cc-by-4.0



The "Lebendiger Atlas - Natur Deutschland" (LAND) bundles species observations in Germany to make them available for specialist applications and the general public. Currently, species occurrences of selected data sets are displayed that are publicly available via the portal of the Global Biodiversity Information Facility (GBIF).

## NFDI4Biodiversity Self-Paced Online Course - Research Data Management for Biodiversity Data

Fischer, Marlen, Röder, Juliane, Signer, Johannes, Tschink, Daniel, Weibulat, Tanja, Brand, Ortrun

Published 2025-11-11

Licensed cc-by-sa-4.0



In NFDI4Biodiversity, we developed a self-paced online&nbsp;course on research data management for both students and researchers specializing in biodiversity and environmental sciences.&nbsp;This course is based on the online course HeFDI Data OER: Forschungsdatenmanagement - eine Online-Einf&uuml;hrung of the Hessian Research Data Infrastructures (HeFDI). Developed in collaboration between several partners within NFDI4Biodiversity, this course offers essential domain-specific knowledge in research data management.
We offer&nbsp;this course in different formats to facilitate accessibility and re-usability:


LiaScript course (Markdown, available on GitHub): https://github.com/NFDI4Biodiversity/nfdi4biodiversity-sle


OER section of Marburg University Ilias (LiaScript import, SCORM1.2, certificate for successful completion):&nbsp;https://ilias.uni-marburg.de/goto.php/crs/3219917


Document (text and figure, no questions): https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.10377867


Here, we additionally offer import files to use this course in your local Ilias or Moodle. If you are using a different learning management system (LMS) or if you want to adapt the course, you are free to clone the corresponding GitHub repository, adapt the course, and export it in a format suitable to your needs.
Please note the following characteristics (different from GitHub sourced online version):


SCORM1.2 format


mastery score 100 &ndash; learners need to achieve 100% correct answers in all questions. You can change this value in the Ilias/Moodle course settings after importing the course, or during the LiaScript export.


Unlimited trials to answer questions correctly, but no solution button


This course is subject to regular maintenance and updates. You can find the most up-to-date version on GitHub:&nbsp;https://github.com/NFDI4Biodiversity/nfdi4biodiversity-sle
For inquiries or additional information, please feel free to reach out to us: NFDI4Biodiversity Helpdesk.
Prerequisites - No previous knowledge is required for this course. The chapters build on each other thematically but can also be worked through individually. If information from other chapters is needed, they are linked.
Target audience - Graduate students, PhD students, and researchers in the field of biology and environmental sciences, especially those working with biodiversity data, who are looking for a first introduction to research data management.
Learning objectives - After completing this self-paced course, you will be able to understand the content and purpose of research data management and apply it in the field of biology. The detailed learning objectives are given at the beginning of each chapter.
Content - If you prefer to read, you can access the content of this unit through the text only. However, the embedded videos offer further access to the topic, so those who work well with video explanations will find the content presented here in a different way.
Duration - If you concentrate on reading all the texts, you can expect to spend about 4-5 hours on the course. If you also watch the videos or follow the links, you will need more time. And remember to take breaks.

## Template for the NFDI4Biodiversity & GfÖ Winter School

Röder, Juliane, Fischer, Marlen, Tschink, Daniel, Brand, Ortrun

Published 2023-08-08

Licensed cc-by-4.0



This&nbsp;publication showcases the NFDI4Biodiversity &amp; Gf&Ouml; Winter School 2022, a transformative one-week course program designed to equip young scientists with essential Research Data Management (RDM) skills tailored for biodiversity and environmental data. Organized by the NFDI4Biodiversity consortium, the Winter School&nbsp;fostered&nbsp;data literacy and promoted sustainable research practices within the ecological and environmental science communities.

The publication offers an&nbsp;overview of the Winter School&#39;s structure, curriculum, and highlights. Throughout the course, participants engaged in a series of expert-led lectures and hands-on practical sessions, delivered by renowned academics, data specialists, and research institutions in the biodiversity domain. These sessions covered critical aspects of RDM, encompassing data collection, curation, analysis, visualization, and long-term preservation.

The curriculum extensively explored state-of-the-art software tools for data handling, including Jupyter notebook, R, Python, RightField, and OpenRefine. By providing hands-on training with these tools, the Winter School empowered participants to enhance their data management workflows and produce robust research outcomes.

Targeting PhD students&nbsp;and Early-Career Researchers, the Winter School nurtured the next generation of scientists, equipping them with the necessary skills to navigate the challenges posed by increasingly complex and diverse ecological datasets.

By sharing insights, lessons learned, and practical guidance, this publication serves as an invaluable reference and guide for organizers, educators, and researchers seeking to create impactful and inclusive training programs in research data management for biodiversity and related studies.

The Winter School&#39;s legacy lies not only in the skills acquired by participants but also in the&nbsp;collaboration&nbsp;across the biodiversity and environmental science community. It exemplifies the commitment of NFDI4Biodiversity to advance data-driven research and&nbsp;contribute to a more sustainable and biodiverse planet.

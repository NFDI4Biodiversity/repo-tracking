## Historical Animal Observation Records by Bavarian Forestry Offices (1845)

Rehbein, Malte, Escobari Vargas, Andrea Belen, Fischer, Sarah, Güntsch, Anton, Haas, Bettina, Matheisen, Giada, Perschl, Tobias, Wieshuber, Alois, Engel, Thore

Published 2024-12-13

Licensed cc-by-4.0



In 1845, under the scientific direction of Andreas Wagner, the Bavarian government recorded the occurrence of 44 selected vertebrate species across the entire country. To this end, Wagner had a survey questionnaire sent to all 119 forestry offices in the state. The foresters' responses were now systematically recorded and analyzed for the first time. This data set represents the result of this survey. Among other things, it contains 5,467 geo-coded animal observation data.
The data is the result of an interdisciplinary collaboration between scientists from the Chair of Computational Humanities at the University of Passau, the Directorate General of the Bavarian State Archives Munich, the German Centre for Integrative Biodiversity Research (iDiv) Halle-Jena-Leipzig, the Center for Biodiversity Informatics and Collection Data Integration at the Botanical Garden Berlin, and the NFDI4Biodiversity consortium.

The "Lebendiger Atlas - Natur Deutschland" (LAND) bundles species observations in Germany to make them available for specialist applications and the general public. Currently, species occurrences of selected data sets are displayed that are publicly available via the portal of the Global Biodiversity Information Facility (GBIF). ## Mobilisation of Historical Animal Observation Data - The Bavarian Survey of 1845

Rehbein, Malte, Engel, Thore, Matheisen, Giada, Hörl, Lina, Haas, Bettina, Perschl, Tobias, Lambsdorff, Felix

Published 2024-05-13

Licensed cc-by-4.0



In 1845, a survey was conducted across all Bavarian forestry districts. The foresters in charge were required to complete a tabular form documenting the animal species present within their specific forest district. The project's objective is to render this historical observational data accessible for biodiversity research purposes.

## NFDI4Biodiversity - Status Report Use Case Projects

Gemeinholzer, Birgit, Frenzel, Mark, Bonn, Aletta, Fischer, Sarah, Holetschek, Jörg, Engel, Thore, Friedrichs-Manthey, Martin, Schomburg, Christoph, Ebert, Barbara

Published 2023-09-07

Licensed cc-by-4.0



Strengthening Germany&#39;s Biodiversity Data Network: User Engagement in NFDI4Biodiversity

The availability of biodiversity data&nbsp;is vital for informed decisions on sustainable use and conservation of biodiversity. NFDI4Biodiversity focuses on enabling biodiversity data exchange among stakeholders.

In collaboration with 50 partner institutions, NFDI4Biodiversity addresses diverse biodiversity facets, driven by partner needs. This report covers a 2.5-year user engagement phase, emphasizing 26 use cases adhering to FAIR principles for data mobilization and integration. The report highlights challenges in networking due to varying stakeholder needs and the need for integration in the biodiversity data landscape.

This report outlines NFDI4Biodiversity&#39;s efforts to strengthen Germany&#39;s biodiversity data network through user engagement, underscoring the importance of collaboration in biodiversity understanding and conservation.
